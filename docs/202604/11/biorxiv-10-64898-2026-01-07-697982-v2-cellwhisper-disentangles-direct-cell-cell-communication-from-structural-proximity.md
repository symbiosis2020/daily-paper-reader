## 摘要
摘要：直接的、依赖接触的细胞间通讯塑造了组织生理与疾病进程。空间转录组学为推断此类通讯机制提供了独特机遇，但现有少数推断工具因细胞类型分布和基因表达的空间结构而易产生高错误率。我们提出CellWHISPER，一个从单细胞分辨率空间数据中推断接触介导的细胞间通讯（包括间隙连接和配体-受体机制）的统计框架。CellWHISPER通过一种专门设计的假设检验程序实现严格的错误控制，该程序考虑了细胞类型特异性表达和空间组织的混杂效应。其采用的精确闭式显著性评分避免了昂贵的置换检验，并能对大型组织和广泛的信号传导目录进行可扩展分析。为揭示显著的通讯模式，CellWHISPER还包含一个潜在变量模型，将反复出现的模式提炼成细胞类型与信号基因间的互作偏好图谱。应用于小鼠大脑STEREO-seq和Xenium数据集时，CellWHISPER识别出细胞类型特异性的间隙连接耦合，并生成了全面的连接蛋白互作图谱。我们通过实验证实了星形胶质细胞、小胶质细胞和内皮细胞间由连接蛋白43介导的界面——这些是排名最高的预测之一；相比之下，现有方法未能优先识别这些已证实的相互作用。对阿尔茨海默病模型与野生型的差异分析显示星形胶质细胞的耦合得以保留，但小胶质细胞相关的间隙连接通讯增强。总之，CellWHISPER将直接的细胞间通讯从结构邻近性中解耦出来，实现了从空间转录组学中进行统计稳健且计算可扩展的推断，从而发现了组织相关和疾病相关的接触介导信号传导程序。

## Abstract
AbstractDirect, contact-dependent cell-cell communication shapes tissue physiology and disease. Spatial transcriptomics offers a unique opportunity to infer such communication mechanisms, but the few existing tools for such inference are prone to high error rates due to spatial structure in distribution of cell types and gene expression. We present CellWHISPER, a statistical framework for inferring contact-mediated cell-cell communication, including gap junctions and ligand-receptor mechanisms, from single-cell resolution spatial data. CellWHISPER achieves strict error control via a specialized hypothesis-testing procedure that accounts for the confounding effects of cell- type-specific expression and spatial organization. Its use of an exact closed-form significance score obviates expensive permutation testing and enables scalable analysis of large tissues an extensive signaling compendia. To uncover salient communication patterns, CellWHISPER also includes a latent variable model that distills recurrent patterns into maps of mutual preferences among cell types and signaling genes. Applied to mouse brain STEREO-seq and Xenium datasets, CellWHISPER identifies cell type-specific gap-junction coupling and yields a comprehensive connexin-interaction map. We experimentally confirmed Connexin 43-mediated interfaces among astrocytes, microglia and endothelial cells, which were among the highest- ranked predictions; in contrast, existing approaches fail to prioritize these confirmed interactions. Differential analysis of an Alzheimers disease model versus wild-type identifies preserved astroglial coupling but increased microglia-associated gap-junction communication. In summary, CellWHISPER disentangles direct cell-cell communication from structural proximity, enabling statistically robust and computationally scalable inference from spatial transcriptomics, leading to the discovery of tissue- and disease-associated contact-mediated signaling programs.
