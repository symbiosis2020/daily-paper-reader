## 摘要
气道上皮必须在持续适应不断变化的环境条件的同时维持屏障完整性。这要求气道上皮中的关键信号通路在一个严格受控的功能范围内运作。本文表明，果蝇气道上皮稳态依赖于平衡的JAK/STAT信号传导。基础通路活性持续存在于分化的上皮细胞中，对细胞存活、上皮完整性和应激抵抗是必需的。与其在上皮生物学中作为应激响应调节因子的作用一致，包括缺氧、香烟烟雾和寒冷暴露在内的环境应激源会诱导JAK/STAT信号传导。相反，持续的途径激活则与气道重塑相关，其特征是上皮增厚、管腔变窄和细胞组织结构改变。转录组学分析表明，持续激活与一个整合了免疫信号、蛋白质稳态和代谢适应的协调性上皮应激程序相关。与小鼠和人类数据集的跨物种比较表明，该响应的关键方面是保守的。药理学抑制实验证明重塑依赖于通路的持续活性，并且可以在体内部分逆转。总之，我们的研究结果支持一个模型：气道上皮稳态依赖于将JAK/STAT信号传导维持在一个特定的功能范围内。这一"金发姑娘"原则为理解生理和病理条件下上皮应激反应如何保持平衡提供了一个概念框架。

## Abstract
Airway epithelia must maintain barrier integrity while continuously adapting to changing environmental conditions. This requires key signaling pathways in airway epithelia to operate within a tightly controlled functional range. Here, we show that airway epithelial homeostasis in Drosophila depends on balanced JAK/STAT signaling. Basal pathway activity is constitutively present in differentiated epithelial cells and is necessary for cell survival, epithelial integrity and stress resistance. In accordance with this role as a stress responsive regulator in epithelial biology, environmental stressors, including hypoxia, cigarette smoke, and cold exposure, induce JAK/STAT signaling. In contrast, sustained pathway activation is associated with airway remodeling characterized by epithelial thickening, luminal narrowing, and altered cellular organization. Transcriptomic analysis reveals that sustained activation is associated with a coordinated epithelial stress program integrating immune signaling, proteostasis, and metabolic adaptation. Cross-species comparisons with murine and human datasets suggest that key aspects of this response are conserved. Pharmacological inhibition demonstrates that remodeling depends on continued pathway activity and can be partially reversed in vivo. Together, our findings support a model in which airway epithelial homeostasis depends on maintaining JAK/STAT signaling within a defined functional range. This "Goldilocks" principle provides a conceptual framework for understanding how epithelial stress responses are balanced under physiological and pathological conditions.
